This is a music service

This service contains a record collection from discogs.com and a listening history from last.fm. Those things often don't play nicely together. Vinyl records lack the necessary metadata tags. The service tries to reduce the friction as much as possible. Lots of that is about thoughtfully displaying lists of things. For a very small number of people (1) it also helps with updating lists on the internet.

Infrequently asked questions

Do you really update a list on the internet every time you listen to a record?

Yes.

Isn't that a bit OCD?

Yes.

Why do you do that?

Everyone* likes a good list. Ask goodreads about their 90 million users. I didn't keep a list before I discovered vinylscrobbler.com. That service meant it wasn't very much work. After a decade or so it's interesting to spot patterns in your listening history. Try it and come back to me in 10 years.

* some people

Why did you make this?

Before I started this project, I was already using discogs, last.fm and vinylscrobbler.com to do broadly the same thing. Around the time I started this, discogs had recently re-written their collection interface, much to the chagrin of long time users, who were complaining about performance and loss of functionality. At the same time vinylscrobbler was experiencing stability and performance issues of their own. I decided that given how good the discogs and last.fm APIs are, I could knock something up to do what I wanted on a Saturday morning. Then I carried on tweaking it, mostly in 5-10 minute bursts.

In my opinion it's also a quite significantly prettier thing to spend time looking at than either last.fm or discogs.

Why did you call it In a silent way?

I like slightly obtuse names. It's named after a Miles Davis album.

I don't listen to the album very much.

How this works

The code for this service uses the discogs API client to create a local copy of a record collection. A django application makes that collection browsable, searchable and filterable. It uses pylast - a python interface to last.fm to fetch and submit last.fm scrobbles. When new scrobbles arrive or new records are added to the collection we try to link scrobbles to records. This process is not 100% correct, but it's pretty good.
